How to confirm eligibility in three practical moves

Step 1 ‚Äì Run the country test

Start with your registered head‚Äëoffice address, not the nationality of individual founders. If that address sits inside the EU‚Äë27 or in an Associated Country‚Äîthink Israel, the United Kingdom or Switzerland after January 2025‚Äîyou automatically qualify for Horizon Europe money.

PIC Registration Guide

  1. Create an EU Login account if you don't already have one
  2. Access the Funding & Tenders Portal and select "Register Organisation"
  3. Complete the basic information about your organization
  4. Upload either a VAT certificate or company registry extract (less than 6 months old)
  5. Submit and wait for your 9-digit PIC (usually immediate, but can take up to 48 hours)

Programme Matchmaker

Why read this chapter?

You have a promising concept and a rough timeline, yet dozens of EU programmes compete for your attention. A wrong match steals weeks of drafting time. This chapter walks you through a narrative exercise that filters every option until only one or two calls remain ‚Äî the ones whose success criteria match your current maturity and domain.

Context

EU funding looks like a giant buffet table. Horizon Europe alone holds nine thematic clusters, the European Innovation Council opens three separate tracks, Digital Europe sprinkles strategic projects on AI and cybersecurity, LIFE funds green pilots, while InvestEU and CEF throw loans and guarantees into the mix.

Beginners often skim the high-level descriptions, latch on to a buzzword, and dive straight into writing. Evaluators then wonder why a climate-mitigation project arrived in a digital-skills call. A disciplined programme-match process flips the script: instead of forcing your idea into the first familiar label, you test it against objective filters ‚Äî readiness, theme, and funding intensity.

Step 1 ‚Äî Anchor on your maturity, not your ambition

Ambition drives entrepreneurs, but evaluators award points for evidence. The European Commission expresses evidence through the Technology Readiness Level ladder. Start by asking, "What can we show in the lab or in the field today?" If the answer is a white-paper, you sit near the bottom of the ladder. If the answer is a working pilot at a customer site, you climb to the middle. Anything already generating revenue approaches the top.

Case: A spin‚Äëout from Haifa University recently discovered this the hard way. They owned an exciting peptide that killed antibiotic‚Äëresistant bacteria in vitro. Believing the solution world‚Äëchanging, they applied directly to the EIC Accelerator. Reviewers loved the science yet failed them on Proof of Market and Traction. Six months later the team rerouted into EIC Transition, coupled the peptide with a clear pre‚Äëclinical plan, and secured 2.3 million euro on the second attempt. The product did not change; the maturity framing did.
